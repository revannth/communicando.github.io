About Us

Communicando

Communicando is the literary club of CBIT. It was started in 2007 with the aim to give students an opportunity to grow as individuals by enriching their soft skills. The name of the club itself means “come you and I can do” implying that with team spirit and perseverance we can enrich ourselves and make miracles happen. So as to achieve this goal, the students of Communicando come together every few weeks and brainstorm to organise events in English, Hindi and Telugu. The events range from public speaking to creative writing events (poem, essay and story writing) which give the diverse student crowd in CBIT a chance to take a break from strenuous class work and participate in events which let them explore themselves and the world. Not only this but Communicando also organises workshops and invites eminent personalities who impart their skills and help students polish their skills.

CBIT being one of the top city colleges encourages students to excel in both academics and co-curriculars as well, Communicando being one such club which helps the student coordinators from all branches first, second and third years to get better at soft skills such as time management, leadership ,planning and creativity to name a few. Not only this but being a part of Communicando has helped students meet like minded individuals and form life long friendships. Many of the ex-students who have acquired jobs at big companies and masters at the top universities credit being a part of Communicando to their success.

Communicando has exciting plans for the future and the coordinators believe that the sky is the limit. We hope to establish dramatics and a book lovers club in the coming years where in students can meet people with similar interests and enjoy their hobbies with the support of the college.

Literati

Literati is the annual literary fest of CBIT, conducted by Communicando. It takes place in March every year, and every edition of Literati brings with it an exciting and rewarding array of events. There are a number of competitions conducted, and the events are a mix of time-tested classics and innovative new ideas. Literati spans two days, and tests participants for various skills. Literati has, over the years since its inception, been a refreshing break for students from the daily grind of their curriculum. It allows students to explore their talents and find a platform for them, going beyond academic and technical ability. These activities serve the purpose of making students good communicators and teamworkers. Organising the fest itself is no small feat, with weeks of planning, effort, and dedication being put in by the Communicando team.

Communication, writing, speaking, presence of mind, leadership are only few of the qualities that we seek to bring out. Events include several rounds, testing participants in debate, group discussion, elocution, critical thinking, decision-making, general knowledge etc. There are comprehensive standards for judging and evaluating performances, and the prizes are an added incentive. The main attraction of Literati, however, lies in the fact that it allows students to present a side of themselves to themselves and their peers that is rarely seen otherwise. Most importantly, it is a welcome opportunity for enthusiastic students to compete with each other, and have some fun doing it.

Workshops

We at Communicando believe in constantly growing and making ourselves familiar with the upcoming forms of literary art and that’s why have been actively working towards inviting accomplished personalities from various fields to conduct workshops where the participants get to interact and experiment.

In February this year Ms.Sarita Rani and Ms.Anjani Meenakshi from Youth Orators Club of Hyderabad conducted an impactful public speaking workshop for the students of CBIT. The Young Orators Club of Secunderabad is a place where people of all ages, backgrounds, and professions come together to participate in public speaking sessions.The club has been active in promoting the art of oratory in the city, especially among students for over 40 years now.

The speakers spoke about the impact that good orators create on their audience and the importance of staying true to your opinions while speaking, this was followed by an impromptu speaking session followed by brief evaluation!

One of the biggest events of Literati 7.0 was a microfiction workshop conducted by the co founder of Terribly Tiny Tales, Anuj Gosalia. Terribly Tiny Tales which became an internet sensation in 2014 and is now one of the country’s most beloved story telling platform which brings together a diverse pool of authors to create tweet sized stories everyday.

The workshop saw over 250 participants from different fields of life, the participants did not just learn how the the Terribly Tiny Tales are created but also penned down a few Terribly Tiny Tales on their own based on the prompts provided.

We hope to conduct many more such workshops in the near future.

Transcendent

Transcendent, the college newsletter of CBIT produces editions every year covering all the latest happenings in the college and around the world. Apart from the printed editions, Transcendent also has a weekly bulletin that covers every week's highlights on its Facebook page that is open to everybody. The newsletter has five sections: News, Sports, Science and Technology, Editorial and Entertainment, all written, edited and designed by the students.

Not only does this help the students increase their general awareness, but it also serves as a platform to experience journalism, improve writing skills and voice opinions. Transcendent also hopes to connect the student body with the administration more closely. It makes sure that readers have an excellent experience reading our newsletters and come back for more every time.

Transcendent aims to inculcate a sense of awareness and sensitivity to issues among students. It also aims to develop the spirit of writing and reporting, which are skills beneficial to any citizen. Education in its truest sense must go above and beyond technical knowledge, and ensure that its recipients are informed and intelligent citizens. Transcendent aims to play a role in this process.

Recent Events

Communicando has set off this year with a bang. The past few weeks have been exhilarating for us at communicando. We have organized exciting events, each being unique and compelling in its own way.

Poetry is a mesmerizing and beautiful art form. It’s a shame to say that in this modern age, poetry is becoming a lost art. Paddam Pattu Sametha Kattu was a fun filled telugu sametha i.e proverb based game where the participants’ knowledge of the many wise proverbs was tested.

CBITMUN

Back in 2011 CBIT MUN club was started by a group of debate enthusiasts with the motto to set up, encourage and endorse Model United Nations Conferences in the city of Hyderabad. It was Model United Nations Initiative for the first time of its level in the city of Hyderabad under the Literary Club of CBIT, Communicando. As years passed it has not only succeeded in conducting a world class conference every year, with a sharp increase in the number of participants with every edition. Since its inception the club has stood for the quality of debate and hospitality it delivers during the conference.

Model United Nations (also Model UN or MUN) is a student directed academic simulation of the United Nations Organization. An estimated 400 model UN conferences are held annually around the world where students come together with the aim of learning how these assemblies function and also to interact and work with people who have the potential to be change leaders of the next generation. Most MUN participants have demonstrated in the past that they can become great leaders in politics, law and even education. The participants addressed as “delegates” role-play diplomats while representing a country of their choice, investigate international issues, debate, deliberate, consult on this forum and then arrive at a comprehensive consensus to real world problems.

CBITMUN 2016 shattered all the standing records. With a delegate count of over 310 and a footfall of almost 500 people, the 6th edition had etched itself a niche of its own. But the delegate count wasn't all that it had offered. It withheld the traditions and stayed loyal to the roots of the club. After 5 successful editions, such a feat is beyond praise. Added to that, this edition gave back to the society by spending a day at the Devarakonda Basti School and brightening the day of the kids by giving them some basic everyday essentials like a pair of slippers, notebooks, stationery and candies. The Training Team Initiative taught the students of RockWell International the essence of an MUN. Every year, our club comes up with innovative ways of improving the culture of MUNs and with every edition we have scaled up the standards in terms of magnitude
